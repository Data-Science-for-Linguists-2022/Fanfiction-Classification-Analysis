Chapter Text

"You all... can't see him?"

"It does sound like the footsteps are coming from that area..." Zenitsu cupped his ears. "But I don't see anyone there..." Tanjiro's eyebrows curled upwards in confusion and he turned back to point at the source once more.

"But he's right- ah... He vanished."

"Are you feeling okay, Gonpachiro? Have a shiny acorn I found."

"Uh. thanks..."

"I'll check you for a fever when we get home, okay, Tanjiro?" He looked distressed by your words.

"Uh?! But I really saw someone though! He looked... just like my..." He trailed off and puzzled the ground beneath him. "That does sound crazy if I say it..."

"Maybe you saw a spirit." You said optimistically, trying to brighten his mood. "That has been known to happen this time of year. Lets keep heading towards the other entrance and maybe we'll see him again." Zenitsu let out a whimper from behind you.

"Do we have to? We can go back to the festival instead right? I'll win you a plush toy, F/N-chan. I promise! One of the great big ones!" You gently snickered at his offer before pulling him along by the hand.

"Pff. Come on, Zenitsu."

As you pressed on you noticed more clusters of decorated graves, fresher flowers and little ornamental keepsakes, which meant you must've been getting close to your destination.

"Shouldn't be much farther now." You informed them.

"Hey..." Inosuke halted his movement and took in his surroundings. "Why are there mirrors by some of the graves?" Tanjiro lifted a finger, excited to share something he knew, as he wasn't used to being the intellectual one on any given topic unless it had to do with coal or homesteading.

"Urokodaki told me demons cant follow spirits through mirrors. So it's a safe place for them." Zenitsu's eyes became wide as saucepans at that.

"Do you mean to tell me demons are gonna be a problem for me even AFTER I die?!"

"Not if you use the mirrors apparently." You chuckled at the blonde's mortified face.

"I thought that mirrors were meant to trap the deceased at the gravesite where they belong, so they don't wander around..." He mumbled grumpily in response, earning a burning question from Inosuke.

"...What if it breaks?"

"Don't even think about it."

"Huh? That's odd..." Tanjiro had stopped in front of a full-length mirror leaning between a headstone and a tree.

"What's odd?" You asked.

"Why... is this mirror empty?"

"What are you talking about, Tanjiro?"

You observed as he reached out his hand through the frame, only to bump his fist on the glass.

"Ah? What the..." He waved his hand in front of it and you approached, stepping up behind him. Peering over Tanjiro's shoulder you balked in the face of what you saw. Though your hand was resting on his arm the mirror's image showed no trace of the boy in front of you. Your hand simply hung in mid air, sense of touch and sight conflicting.

"Uh... guys?" You called to the other two with a wavering voice. "There's something wrong with this mirror."

Inosuke was the first to come investigate. He took off his mask behind Tanjiro, waved a hand in front of the reflective surface, then leaned far over to stare blankly at the back of it.

"It's a... trick. Right?" You needed reassurance that it was just a very captivating prank and not something terrifyingly supernatural.

"Yeah... Like those birthday candles they sell at the train station that don't blow out right away..."

Just then Inosuke wordlessly dragged Tanjiro to another small mirror a few headstones away.

"Ah, Inosuke! You don't have to pull so hard."

"What are you doing?" Zenitsu asked in an irritated sort of fashion. He was feeling impatient and jittery, the way a field mouse must feel when being watched by a hawk, and wanted to get out of this graveyard as fast as possible.

"He's not in this one either!" Inosuke's voice grew more manic.

"What's happening?" You asked shakily and Zenitsu finally stomped over.

"Would you hurry up? ...WAH! WHERE'S YOUR REFLECTION?!" He jumped back, gawking at Tanjiro like he was going to be eaten. "HAVE YOU BEEN A GHOST ALL THIS TIME AND NEVER TOLD US?!"

"There's no way that's possible, Zenitsu." You held up your hands, trying to calm him down. But then, in an unexpected display of emotion, Inosuke abruptly burst into tears and latched onto Tanjiro.

"UWAAHHH! I knew I shouldn't have taken all those rice balls you gave me! My subordinate is too weak to fight the deeeaaaddd! It's my fault! I'm a horrible leader!"

"ARE WE GETTING PULLED INTO THE SPIRIT WORLD?!" Zenitsu latched onto you the same way Inosuke tackled Tanjiro. "I TOLD YOU WE SHOULDN'T HAVE DISTURBED THE DEAD! F/N-CHAN AT LEAST KISS ME GOODBYE! We can die on the same side of the river and be together! I don't wanna die alooone!"

"Zenitsu, get down. I'm sure there's a perfectly reasonable explanation for this."

"Uwehheheh..." Inosuke slipped down to his knees and Tanjiro patted his head in an attempt to console him.

"I wonder if this has something to do with the fortune teller that touched my hand earlier." An epiphany washed over both of you and you realized that must have been the case.

"Nobody touch Tanjiro!" Zenitsu blurted without thinking. "What if the curse is contagious?!"

Inosuke let go of the water swordsman immediately, frantically trying to shake the invisible force off his hands.

"Uh?! WEH?! UWAAAAAAGHH!"

"Zenitsu, you're not helping!" You scolded his poor timing, and Tanjiro finally spoke up to quell the mayhem.

"Alright, everyone just calm down for a second! ...I know you're scared, and we don't know why this is happening. But it's just a reflection I suppose. Assuming the worst and this is some kind of curse, at least she didn't take anything that I really needed."

"You're handling this a little too well..." Zenitsu deadpanned to Tanjiro before finally letting go of you.

"Well, if her powers are anything like a blood demon art, the effects will wear off after a while." He thoughtfully touched a fingertip to his lips. "If it doesn't... I suppose I can just have someone else cut my hair for me the rest of my life... considering the fact that I wont be able to see myself. I can always compensate them for the inconvenience. ...So as long as I'm not in any pain and it doesn't keep me from trying to help Nezuko, I'm not terribly worried about the curse."

"If that isn't the most stupidly optimistic thing I've ever- hwuhh...."

"What's wrong Zenitsu?"

"It's the footsteps again..." His voice came out trembly. "I think they're... dancing. F/N-chan... is he standing behind me? I'm frozen in fear and can't look!"

"You're hearing things." Inosuke asserted flatly, returning to his feet. Tanjiro squinted towards the top right corner of the mirror, then turned to look past Zenitsu's rigid shivering form.

"Ah, you're right! He's there again!"

"BWAAAH!"

Before you could respond Tanjiro took off towards the denser thicket of trees, following nothing into the absolute and utter blackness.

"Wait! Tanjiro! Come back! Don't go off alone! It could be dangerous!" But he was already zigzagging through the trees faster than you could keep up.

"F/N-chan, don't leave me!" Zenitsu and Inosuke ran after you as the distance between you and Tanjiro widened.

"Tanjiro!"

...

At last the other two caught up, seeing you glance around chaotically in search of your companion. But all you could see was more trees.

"Where'd he go?" Inosuke asked.

"I lost him..." You fell into despair, trying to catch your breath. "I'm worried. I didn't want to get separated before we knew what's going on with him." Zenitsu let out an indignant shout.

"AAAGH! And he ran off with Nezuko-chan too! What was he thinking?!"

"I think he went this way." You moved to take off into a run but Zenitsu's hand caught your wrist..

"Be careful, F/N-chan. You need to stay close to me. I can't risk losing you. ...Don't forget, we're in trouble too. What if that invisible person tries to attack us?! We can't see him!"

"I think if he wanted to he had several opportunities to get the drop on us. It's possible he knows Tanjiro, so he only revealed his presence to him. Assuming he's a ghost, that is."

"But that doesn't explain his missing reflection." Inosuke brought up an important point.

"That's true..."

"It's all that fortune teller's fault!" Zenitsu cried out in frightened agitation. "She put some kind of spell on Tanjiro! And I bet we're next!"

"Then lets go beat her up until she fixes him!"

"You cant solve all your problems with your fists, moron."

"Well I can't just leave my subordinate unguarded!"

"...What are you doing?" Inosuke's sudden saunter of confidence in a random direction piqued your curiosity.

"What is he doing?" Zenitsu mumbled to you.

"I'm gonna ask the dead people to find him."

You and Zenitsu looked at each other, but didn't have a better idea, so you stayed silent and followed him. Passing by a few patches of graves Inosuke made a handful of quick and jagged changes in direction.

"How are you going to do that?" You asked and he shrugged.

"Easy. I'm just gonna ask them."

Just outside the thicker woods was a cluster of headstones, and this is where Inosuke chose to stop, a few meters ahead of you.

"This one feels like a good batch." Zenitsu was skeptical of his assertion.

"How do you even-"

"Shut up, I'm busy." The boar-headed young man took in a deep breath, filling his chest with air and stretching his arms out wide.

"I wonder how he's planning on contacting them." You whispered to Zenitsu. "Do you think one of his beast breathing techniques can- "

"CONTACTING ALL SMELLY DEAD PEOPLE!"

"ARE YOU INSANE?! YOU CAN'T SAY STUFF LIKE THAT, YOU IDIOT!"

"We're off to a great start..." You involuntarily smacked a palm to your forehead in exhaustion.

"THIS IS YOUR LEADER SPEAKING!"

"WE'RE ALL GONNA DIE!" Zenitsu shrieked but Inosuke continued his abrasive speech.

"I ask that you take us back to our friend, Gonpachiro!"

"They're not gonna know who that-"

"He wears GREEN!" Inosuke increased his volume to drown out the naysayer. "And has a scar on his forehead! That's how you'll know its him!"

"Inosuke, I don't think this is gonna work..." You admitted delicately.

"And don't bother the demon girl he's carrying! She's nice." He added one last detail to his short rude message, earning Zenitsu's thick sarcasm.

"Oh, that's REALLY gonna convince them to- HWUH?!" A visible shiver ran down Zenitsu's spine and there was a notable pick up in the wind. It blew your hair in all directions and the dew-shining grass caught the moonlight in rapidly changing patterns. "I think we made them angry! Should we run?!"

"No..." You extended a hand downwind. "Look!"

"I don't believe it..."

"How... is the grass glowing like that...?"

"Its making a path." Your fearful companion observed in awe.

"Ooh, shiny!" Inosuke began grabbing handfuls of the glowing grass like he was picking beautiful flowers.

"We're supposed to follow it, idiot."

....

"Mmm... Ow..." Tanjiro groaned on the ground, having taken a considerable tumble down a steep hill. He then noticed his sister sprawled out in front of him, blinking up at the harvest moon. "Ah! Nezuko! I'm so sorry! That must've been such a rude awakening. I had to jump over a few headstones to keep up with his footwork. I should have watched where I was going... I didn't hurt you, did I?"

"Mm-mmm." The lovely girl shook her head casually.

"Good." The elder brother sighed in relief.

"Mm?" Nezuko glanced around the empty space around her, confused, as she was sure she heard the voices of her friends not that long ago. Tanjiro caught on to what she was thinking.

"Oh, the others? I got so distracted that I must have left them behind." He frowned, guilt setting in. "I shouldn't have done that... They're probably really worried considering all that's happened tonight... I can tell you about it as we look for them.

Do you want to get back in your box?"

"Mm-mm."

"No? You want to walk with me for a bit?" When she nodded a pleasant little 'mm' Tanjiro's warm smile returned. "Thanks, Nezuko. You always know when I need some company. I'm feeling a little lost... not in the literal sense, but... I saw someone a little while ago.

This is going to sound... really outlandish... but... the man I saw... he was wearing father's regalia, and dancing his exact Kagura. He even smelled like I remember father smelling. But... I can't help but think now that... it was just wishful thinking that tricked my senses. I miss them so much..." His inner sorrow returned, and Nezuko patted his head in a gesture of comfort. "But it couldn't have been, right? They're gone... He's gone."

Suddenly a blazing bright light came streaming through the trees. It was white and consuming and seemed to be shining from all directions!

"Agh! What is that light?! The sun?!" In a fit of panic he jumped in front of his sister. "Get behind me, Nezuko!" After a few seconds he noted his sister was still touched by the glow and remained unharmed. That was when a realization dawned on him. "Wait, it's too early for sunrise..." He squinted around, trying to find the source of the light, but to no avail. And it was then that a disembodied voice, followed by a deep and lingering echo called out his name.

"Tanjiro..."

"Ah?" There was a familiarity in that voice, but he couldn't pinpoint where he knew it from right away. "Who's there?"

...

A vibrant luminescent green lit your path on the tips of many blades of grass, not unlike the glow of fireflies in the muggy summer months. The three of you had broken into a run, Zenitsu now leading you by sound. Finally you saw two familiar figures kneeling alone in a clearing.

"Ah! We found them!" You called out in relief. "Nezuko's awake now too!"

Zenitsu slid down the side of the steep rocky hillside in a much more poised manner than you thought him capable of.

"Wah! Why are you crying, Nezuko-chaan!?" He began to tear up immediately out of empathy, and Nezuko shook her head and tried to answer him.

"Mm-mmmm."

When you got up close you noted tears glistening on Tanjiro's cheeks as well.

"Are you two alright?! Tell us what happened, Tanjiro!"

"It was him... father..." Tanjiro's voice quivered as he struggled to hold back a waterfall of emotion.

"Huh?"

"He walked for days to find me... and led me back to our family. My brothers and sisters... mom..." You listened attentively to him, and he wiped away tears with the heel of his palm. "She said she was proud of me... It really was a gift! It wasn't a curse..."

"Are there any demons in the afterlife?!" Zenitsu had impeccable timing, as always. "Did you ask them?!"

"No..." Tanjiro replied. "They're fully at peace... They came to find me... and I found out... we're the ones who are lost.

It takes our whole lives... to find our way home."

The End
